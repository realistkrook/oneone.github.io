What can a website access?

Websites can access some information directly from your visit, and they can also request permission to access more. Here's a breakdown:

What a website can access directly:

  • IP Address: Your IP address reveals your general location (city or region) and can be used to track your activity across different websites. However, it doesn't pinpoint your exact location.

What a website can request permission to access:

  • Location: Websites can ask for permission to access your precise location (GPS coordinates).
  • Device Information: Some websites may request access to your device's microphone, camera, or other features.
  • Browser Data: Websites can access cookies and browsing history stored in your browser.

What this website knows about you:

Now the approximate location may not be exactly, however hackers can link your IP Address to data breaches, and therefore get your password if you reuse passwords.
